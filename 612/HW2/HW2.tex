\section*{Problem 1}
The variation of the action is given by
\begin{align*}
    \delta S &= \cintd{t_1}{t_2}{t} \left[p\delta\dot{q} + \dot{q}\delta p - \pdv{H}{q}\delta q - \pdv{H}{p}\delta p\right] \\
    &= \cintd{t_1}{t_2}{t} \left[-\dot{p}\delta q + \dot{q}\delta p - \pdv{H}{q}\delta q - \pdv{H}{p}\delta p\right] \\
    &= \cintd{t_1}{t_2}{t} \left[\dot{q}\delta p - \pdv{H}{p}\delta p - \dot{p}\delta q - \pdv{H}{q}\delta q \right] \\
    &= \cintd{t_1}{t_2}{t} \left[\left(\dot{q} - \pdv{H}{p}\right)\delta p - \left(\dot{p} + \pdv{H}{q}\right)\delta q\right],
\end{align*}
where the assumption that $\delta q(t_1) = \delta q(t_2) = 0$ was used to integrate by parts in the second line. Demanding stationary action, we find
\[
\delta S = 0 \implies
\begin{array}{l}
    \dot{q} - \pdv{H}{p} = 0  \\
    \dot{p} + \pdv{H}{q} = 0
\end{array} \implies
\begin{array}{l}
    \dot{q} = \pdv{H}{p} \\
    \dot{p} = -\pdv{H}{q}
\end{array}
\]
